Tech Campuses of Silicon Valley

Samsung


Samsung is a departure from the others featured thus far: it is an older company founded in South Korea that has developed offices in the valley in an attempt to tap into the development and culture that has risen up. In a move of confidence, the foreign company has constructed a modern, beautiful office complex that takes into account not only the comfort desires of its employees but also the architectural stylings that are hard to find thus far in the development of Silicon Valley. The main building resembles a stack of cell phones, which Samsung is famous for, the entire campus features bright colors that are reminiscent of Google's designs, there is all the outdoor space that Apple's development is seeking to integrate, among other features.


The designs are not without thought to functionality: the offices are set up so that employees are allowed to choose where on the campus they would like to work, with sections placed for noise and conveniences based on the preferences of the individual. For example, employees that prefer a more social, faster paced work environment can choose a location near the parking and dining facilities, increasing the traffic near their work centers, still others can place themselves in a corner on the top floor of a remote building where they can work in near silence. The development of this style of campus is Samsung's attempt to attract new talent from the USA, driving more innovation and injecting some Silicon Valley stylings into the slightly more traditional corporate structure of Korea.
