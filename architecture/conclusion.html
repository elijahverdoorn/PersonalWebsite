Tech Campuses of Silicon Valley

Conclusion

For the modern engineer, especially in the software area, there is a strong drive towards working in the bay area. Since these engineers drive the innovation that both small and large companies are built on corporate structures are being modified for the sake of attracting engineers, and spaces are no exception. The shiny-and-new facilities that we fixate on are not without flaws, but following research in the area of team development and efficiency can yield gains for companies looking to maximize their employee's potential. This departure from the older mindsets of conformity and service to the corporate elite draws inspiration from the 60s eras of freedom and counter-culture and was nurtured along by the leaders from Stanford University. The university was not only used as a model for culture, but additionally was used as a physical model for how to build a workplace that is conducive to the development of ideas and product, creating spaces that until recently resembled university campuses. As of late, the same companies that once upheld the ideals of minimal waste have become more bold, beginning construction that will shape the face of the company and their interaction with the public for the foreseeable future.
