Tech Campuses of Silicon Valley

Introduction


Silicon Valley has earned the reputation as a developer's paradise, a place where ideas are made reality and projects get funded with the blink of an eye. Technologists from all corners of the world flock to the bay area to work, some at small startups and others at a larger, more established firm. Recent research suggests that one's working environment is key to productivity and job fulfillment, including the building in which one does their work. In an industry that relies so heavily on recruiting top talent, and allowing for the already established idea that capturing more of a developer's time is more productive than hiring a new team member, the companies that call Silicon Valley home are keen on making their offices as attractive as possible in an effort to retain their employees. Here I seek to explore a brief history of the construction and creation that has led to the current state of the area, focusing on notable workplaces.
