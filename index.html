About Me

My passion for creating beautiful and functional programs started in High School, when I discovered Java and the amazing products that I can create with code. I'm a student at St. Olaf College, where I study Computer Science and Mathematics. While here, I've become involved in activities that have given me opportunities to develop personally and professionally. I have the opportunity to lead my peers in my work with student government, where I serve as Webmaster. This role gives me the chance to put my programming skills on display while developing myself as a mentor and manager of junior web developers. Other roles that I have on campus include my work with the Digital Scholarship Center, where I lead teams to develop workshops and programs for faculty and students revolving around technology and it's integration into the humanities. These two experiences have taught me a lot about the liberal arts perspective on computing.

The liberal arts perspective is one that I look to take into my future work - using my technical skills and bredth of knowledge to help others. I have seen the spark that the right solution to a complicated problem can have on a user, and strive to provide that solution for my clients. I'm always looking for the next project that I can get involved in, and would love to become a part of your team.

Members

St. Olaf uses WordPress as a content management system, and needed a way to display the members of various organizations. I built a plugin for WordPress to do just that. The requesters were so pleased that they came back to me to ask for the addition of functionality to manage the various office hours that are held. After adding these features, the plugin was deployed and is used in production today.

Web-Based Mobile Display of 3D Models

In a month-long project, I built a client-server system to render a large 3D model of the interior of a building on the web. Attention was taken to the potential of mobile display, responsiveness, and user experience. Testing was done across platforms, and the results were published in the proceedings of the 2016 Midwestern Instructional Computing Symposium. This project also lead to a speaking engagement sponsored by the Department of Mathematics, Statistics, and Computer Sciences at St. Olaf College in April 2016.

Read the Paper!

Training Android Developers

In collaboration with the St. Olaf department of Mathematics, Statistcs, and Computer Sciences, I developed a curriculum called Training Android Developers that took computer science students with no knowledge of Java through the basics of the Java language, then through a series of lessons designed to instruct in the basics of creating Android applications. I then used this curriculum to teach CSCI284: Mobile Computing Applications to students in the spring of 2016.

Tile-Based Game Engine

A game engine I made for the final project of CSCI251: Software Design at St. Olaf College. The engine is written in C++ and uses the SFML library, this project taught me about working with source control, building applications in teams, managing documentation, and limiting feature creep to meet deadlines. Source code can be seen on GitHub.

Model ALU

The Arithmatic Logic Unit (ALU) is an integral part of the CPU. In St. Olaf College's CSCI241: Hardware Design, an assingment was given to write an implementation of the processes of the ALU in a high-level programming language. I took the opportunity to introduce myself to rapid prototyping and minimum viable products, completing the assignment efficiently. My solution, written in C++, can be viewed on GitHub.

View the Code!

Assembly Language Exploration Project

Assembly languages are low-level programming languages that present unique challenges to programmers. Every time I read about the Apollo moon missions, I am reminded that the code that helped send those people to the moon was written in a language very similar to the as88 assembly that I used in this application. For the final project in CSCI241: Hardware Design, the class was tasked to create a text-analysis and string-substitution program.
